=== EXECUTIVE SUMMARY ===

This internship report documents my 12-week experience at Wintershall Dea GmbH,
one of Europe's leading independent natural gas and crude oil producers. The internship
focused on geological data analysis, reservoir characterization, and operational support
for North Sea gas production facilities.

=== COMPANY BACKGROUND ===

Wintershall Dea is the largest German crude oil and natural gas producer.
The company operates production facilities in several countries including:
- Germany (onshore and offshore North Sea)
- Norway (North Sea and Norwegian Sea)
- Denmark (Danish North Sea sector)
- United Kingdom (UK North Sea sector)
- Argentina, Mexico, and other regions

The company was formed through the merger of Wintershall (founded 1894) and DEA

=== TECHNICAL WORK COMPLETED ===

1. RESERVOIR CHARACTERIZATION - BRAVO FIELD

Location: Southern North Sea, UK sector
Water Depth: 32 meters
Discovery Year: 1976
Current Production: Natural gas from Permian Rotliegend sandstone

I analyzed production data from 8 production wells and 3 injection wells.
Key findings:
- Average porosity: 15-18% in reservoir zones
- Permeability range: 50-500 mD
- Net-to-gross ratio: 0.65-0.78
- Original gas in place (OGIP): 42 billion cubic meters (estimated)
- Current recovery factor: 68%

2. SEISMIC INTERPRETATION PROJECT

Using Petrel software, I assisted in re-interpreting 3D seismic data covering
120 square kilometers of the Bravo field area. Key activities:
- Identified 3 previously unmapped faults with throws of 15-45 meters
- Correlated seismic horizons with well log data from 11 wells
- Mapped potential bypassed pay zones in the Lower Rotliegend
- Generated structural maps showing top reservoir structure

3. WELL LOG ANALYSIS

I processed and interpreted wireline log data from:
- 4 recent appraisal wells (drilled 2023-2024)
- 6 legacy wells requiring re-interpretation

Log suites analyzed:
- Gamma Ray (GR)
- Neutron Porosity (NPHI)
- Density (RHOB)
- Sonic (DT)
- Resistivity (RT, RXO)
- Nuclear Magnetic Resonance (NMR) - 2 wells

4. PRODUCTION OPTIMIZATION STUDY

Collaborated with production engineers to identify opportunities for
gas rate enhancement. Analysis included:
- Well performance curves for 8 producing wells
- Nodal analysis to identify tubing constraints
- Compression requirements for declining wells
- Artificial lift evaluation for condensate-rich wells

Recommended interventions:
- Well Bravo-12: Install velocity string to reduce liquid loading
- Well Bravo-07: Evaluate gas lift implementation
- Well Bravo-03: Re-perforate upper zone (identified from seismic)
